Our Color Palette

Primary Color Palette

Green #83BE6C

Throughout most of our app, the soft shades of green to represent money and financing as well as nature to relieve stress in managing expenses. It's used in most of our UI, buttons and app pages for viewing that's easy for the user's eyes, and the distinct shades of green give off a friendly and reliable energy.

Secondary Color Palette

Dark Blue #15243C

Dark blue is mainly used in our components to contrast with the lighter colors and stand-out for visibility while still remaining a calm color. In most components it's used in, it represents buttons that have been clicked or function in an opposite way to the green buttons.

Tertiary Color Palette

Dark Green #274B28

This was used in multiple components throughout all of the wireframes so that it contrasts with the primary green. It was also used in the wireframe's dark mode to fit in with MoolahMate's color style.

Accent Colors

Accent Blue #B2D4E6

Accent White #F3EDEA

These lighter colors are used on large areas on the app pages to create the same calm and user-friendly aesthetic that we intended for the green color palette.

Accessibility Colors

Error #CD533B

Warning #FFB647

Success #28E256

Neutral #A3A3A3

These accessibility colors were chosen for different actions the user makes letting them know what the button or page means, like red for error or light green for success. They were also toned down in saturation to align with the main colors of the app.

Color Usage examples

Color Contrast

Color contrast was tested using Coolors and altered to ensure that the colors met accessibility standards. The main text that will go on the page screens will use the dark-color text on light-color screens. Light-color text on dark-color screens are used on minor components and on dark mode.
